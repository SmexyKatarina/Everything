On the Subject of Everything

We’re in the endgame now.

  • On the module is a empty panel, as well as five buttons on the top with icons of modules on them.
  • The first four buttons in order, will have these icons except for the last one. It will have a number written on it that will change over time.
    • If the text is RED, it means that there are no met criteria to open the final panel.
    • If the text is YELLOW, it means that there is one met criteria to open the final panel.
  • The criteria that need to be met in order for the last panel to be unlocked are: Solve all solvable modules* and Solve all panels on the module.
  • Clicking on one of the four buttons that have the icons will place its respective objects upon the empty panel showing information for it's respective module in the list below.
  • If a panel is already solved (green coloring), it means that there is still information you have to gather from it BUT there is nothing to be interacted with to get your digit.
  • Each of the four buttons with icons will give a digit. Make sure that you have not when into the final phase without getting this information as it will not give you a free way out in order to check.
  • The four digits are obtain by their respective manuals below. This four-digit number will be used for the final panel and will be referred to as FF (short for, Final Four).
  • If you strike on the final panel, the other panels will open back up and will be allowed to switch between all five of them.
  • Only modules that are in this manual will appear on Everything.
* Some modules are ignored by Everything.

On the Subject of Wires

The panel will appear as a normal Wires module.

If this panel is one of the first four:

Figure out which wire needs to be cut from this panel by using the original Wires manual. Obtain the position of the wire that needs to be cut from top bottom. This will be the digit for this panel.

This panel cannot be interacted with.

If this panel is the final panel:

All of the wires will be fully black; however, the wires can still be felt on the module, There will always be six wires on the module.

Take the sum of all the digits in the FF, modulo that by 17 and add 1. Count down the number of rules in the Wires manual with the topmost rule being 1. Cut the wire that the rule tells you to.

Keep in mind of the following statements:

  • If the rule refers to the color of the wire, ignore the color condition and cut the wire it refers to. (e.g. "Cut the last blue wire" actually means "Cut the last wire")
  • If the rule refers to an edgework condition, the edgework condition must be true in order to cut that wire. If the edgework condition is not true, proceed to the next rule in order.

On the Subject of The Button

The panel will appear as a normal The Button module but the strip is missing.

If this panel is one of the first four:

To determine the digit for this panel, hold the button down and refer the the strip color that appears below the button. The color will be used in the table below. To not strike on this panel, release the button when the last digit is equal to the number from the table below. If it's released at the wrong time, the module will issue a strike and the strip will NOT change color.

Color Digit Color Digit
Red 1 Orange 6
Yellow 5 Green 9
Blue 4 Indigo 3
Violet 7 White 0
Gray 2 Black 8

If this panel is the final panel:

Take the digital root of the FF and call it X. Follow the instructions in The Button's manual following additional rules below.

  • If the button should be tapped, it must be tapped when the last digit of the timer is equal to X.
  • If the button should be held and released, take the number normally associated with the strip color and add that to X. Then, take the last digit of the sum and release when the last digit of the timer is equal to that digit.
  • A strike will not affect the state of the button.

On the Subject of Keypad

The appearance of this panel will depend on when it is being shown.

If this panel is one of the first four:

The panel will appear to look like a normal Keypad. Use the original Keypad manual to find out which order to press the buttons in. Each button will have a value in reading order from 1-4. Concatenate all the correct button presses into a four-digit base-5 number.

Convert this number into base-10 and modulo by 10 and that is the digit for this panel.

This panel cannot be interacted with.

If this panel is the final panel:

The panel will appear with a 4x4 grid of Keypad symbols. Each button will have a symbol on it from the various columns of Keypad.

Take each digit of the FF and modulo by 6 and add 1. If any digit is the same as the last digits, add 1 until it isn't, wrapping around back to 1 if needed.

The new four-digit number from left to right will correspond to their individual columns from left to right. Press ALL symbols from the first number from top to bottom and proceed likewise for the other three digits.

Once all of the buttons have been properly pressed, the module will solve.

If an incorrect button is pressed, a strike will be issued.

On the Subject of Simon Says

The panel will show a Simon Says diamond dependent on when it is being shown. It will also show a strike counter in the top right.

If this panel is one of the first four:

The diamond is colored. This panel will be interacted with in order to solve the panel. Use the rules from the original Simon Says manual and solve the panel.

When it refers to the strikes, use the strike counter in the top right of the diamond. Convert colors to their respective numbers: Blue => 1, Red => 2, Yellow => 3, Green => 4.

Concatenate the sequence of numbers into a three- to five-digit base-5 number. Then, convert this number from base-5 to base-10. The last digit of this number is the digit for this panel.

Failing to click the correct button will issue a strike. Inputs into the panel will be reset after 3 seconds.

If this panel is the final panel:

The diamond is uncolored. Take the digital root of the FF and use that number with table below to figure out what the colors of the diamond are.

Taking the order from the table below, use this to place colors onto the diamond, in clockwise order, starting from the top. Solve the module like a normal Simon Says using the top right number for the number of strikes

Failing to click the correct button will issue a strike. Inputs into the panel will be reset after 3 seconds.

If this panel is one of the first four:

This panel cannot be interacted with.

Digit Words Digit Words

On the Subject of Memory

This panel will look like a normal Memory module.

If this panel is one of the first four:

Solve this panel like a normal Memory module. Concatenate the five pressed labels in order into a five-digit base-5 number.

Convert this number to base-10 and take the last digit of this number and that is the digit for this panel.

This panel is not interactable once it reaches stage five. The panel will also have a delayed panel solve of 30 seconds on the last stage.

If this panel is the final panel:

The display that normally displays the stage number will be blank for each stage. For this panel, you'll need a five-digit number. Use the last digit of the serial number as the fifth digit.

Each of the digits in the five-digit number will each correspond to a stage from left to right. Take each digit and modulo 4 and add 1.

Use the first digit from left to right for the first stage, the second digit for the second stage, etc. Do this until the module is solved.

Pressing an incorrect button will not reset the module nor the stage.

On the Subject of Complicated Wires

This panel will present always with six wires instead of a random amount.

If this panel is one of the first four:

Using the original manual, find out which wires will need to be cut where purple is red and blue. The six wires from left to right will represent a binary number from most to least significant.

Each correct cut will be a 1; otherwise, it will be a 0. Convert this binary number into a base-10 number and take the last digit of this number to be the digit for this panel

This panel is not interactable.

If this panel is the final panel:

All of the wires will be white with no LED and no star. This may or may not be the actual state of the wire.

For this panel, you'll need a six-digit number. To get the fifth and sixth digits, use the first two digits of serial respectively.

Now that you have a six-digit number, use each digit individually in the table below to get the states of the wires from left to right by using the digits from left to right.

Once all correct wires have been cut, the module will solve.

2 Blue 7 White
3 Red, Blue, LED 8 White, LED
4 Red, LED, Star 9 Blue, Star

On the Subject of Wire Sequences

This panel will look like a normal Wire Sequence module.

If this panel is one of the first four:

Go through all of the wire panels and find out which wires, by their respective numbers on left side, that need to be cut using the original Wire Sequences manual tables.

Take all of these numbers and sum them together and take the last digit and add 1. This result is the digit for this panel.

The wires will not be interactable, but the buttons to switch panels without being stuck as a penalty.

If this panel is the final panel:

This final panel will be solved like a normal Wire Sequences module, however there are offsets to the cut tables that need to be calculated.

To calculate the true offset for each of the tables do the following:

  • Red Table: Take the first digit of the FF and add it to the fourth digit of the FF. Take this modulo 10 and add one. This is the true offset.
  • Blue Table: Take the second digit of the FF and add it to the fourth digit of the FF. Take this modulo 10 and add one. This is the true offset.
  • Black Table: Take the third digit of the FF and add it to the fourth digit of the FF. Take this modulo 10 and add one. This is the true offset.

Using these offsets on their respective tables, go down that many rows in the table starting from "First Occurrence" and that is your Starting row or your "First Occurrence".

Solve the panel as you would normally solve a Wire Sequences. If you happen to reach the end of the table, wrap around back to the top and continue.

Trying to go back or to the next panel while wires still need to be cut, will result in a strike. Cutting an incorrect wire will result in a strike.

Once all correct wires have been cut, the module will solve.

On the Subject of Morse Code

The module will appear with a Morse Light.

If this panel is one of the first four:

The Morse Light will begin to transmit morse, all dashes and dots are counted when it is not a dark color. A word will be transmitted from the original Morse Code manual.

Once you have obtained the word, take the frequency and take the digit in the hundredths place (e.g. if the light is flashing "break", the hundredths place is 7). This result is the digit for this panel.

If this panel is the final panel:

The panel will now present an entire interface like a Morse Code module. The light will transmit a four-digit number, add it to the FF. Remove any 0s in this number and multiply each digit together (e.g. 269 => 2*6*9 => 108). Take this result and modulo by 100 and call this number M

Taking M, look at all the frequencies from the original Morse Code manual and find out which frequencies that M is closest to. If M is closest to multiple, use either one of them.

Transmitting an incorrect frequency will issue a strike.

On the Subject of Passwords

This panel will appear to be a Password module missing or not missing its submit button.

If this panel is one of the first four:

The submit button will be missing in this case. Search for the word by using the arrows above and below the letters.

After finding the word, use the table in the original Passwords manual and start from the top left of the table and go in reading order, starting from 1, until you reach your word.

The last digit of the result will be the digit for this panel.

The submit button will me available here but all of the letters will cycle through the whole English alphabet.

Take FF and modulo it by 35 and add 1. The word with this value in the table, in reading order, will be the word to the module.

Submitting an incorrect word will result in a strike.

On the Subject of Maze

This panel will appear like a normal Maze module.

If this panel is one of the first four:

The red triangle and indicators will be missing from the maze. The white LED will only be visible on the maze. A random maze have been been chosen from 1-9, in reading order from the manual.

An unlit LED will will be present at the top right of the maze.

By moving around, figure out which maze you are in. Whenever the LED runs into a wall, the wall will not be highlighted but rather the unlit LED will become a fake strike and under it will show in which direction the wall was hit.

The digit for this panel will be a digit from 1-9 depending on what number the maze is in reading order.

If this panel is the final panel:

The red triangle and white LED will be missing from the maze. The indicators will be present in the maze, tell you which maze it is.

Take FF and split it into 2 pairs of 2. The first two digits being the first pair and the other two being the second pair. In each pair of digits, modulo both digits by 6 and add one to each of them.

The first pair of digits will refer to the position of the white LED. The second pair of digits will refer to the position of the red triangle.

A reset is present in the bottom right in case in a loss of position, which will be reset to the original location of the first pair.

Attempting to cross a wall will result in a strike. The white LED will NOT move positions.
